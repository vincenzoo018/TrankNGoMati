## 5. Technical Notes for the Developer/AI

- **FSM design (1.3.2.2):** Explicitly define states (e.g., Submitted, Endorsed, Accepted, Reviewed, Approved, Returned, Rejected, Released) and a transition table of which roles can move a document between which states. This should be documented separately as your FSM diagram for the methodology chapter.
- **ARTA thresholds (1.3.2.6):** Confirm with your adviser which document types map to the 3-day (simple), 7-day (complex), and 20-day (highly technical) tiers per the Anti-Red Tape Act/RA 11032 — this classification should be a required field at document creation, not inferred automatically, unless OCR/metadata is trained to do so reliably.
- **SMS Gateway (1.3.2.4):** Decide early on a provider (e.g., Semaphore, Globe Labs, ITexMo) since integration and cost differ — this affects both your budget/limitations section and your system architecture diagram.
- **OCR engine (1.3.2.8):** Tesseract OCR or Azure/Google Cloud Vision are common choices for ASP.NET projects; note that cloud OCR APIs may raise a data-privacy consideration worth addressing in your Scope & Limitations, since scanned documents may contain sensitive citizen information.
- **Password-Gated Export (1.3.2.7):** The secondary password should not be the same as the user's login password — treat it as a distinct "export PIN" with its own hashing/storage, and log every attempt.
- Audit Trail entries must be immutable/append-only to satisfy COA record-keeping standards.
- SLA/ARTA countdown calculations should exclude weekends and Philippine holidays.
- Public Tracking Portal must NOT expose internal comments, attachments, or assignee names — status and tracking number only.
- Role-based access control (RBAC) is required before enabling User & Office Management.
